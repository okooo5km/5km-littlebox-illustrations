# Littlebox IP

## Definition

Littlebox (小盒) is a recurring paper-box character for article illustrations. It is not a mascot, sticker, or cute helper. It is a small operator that physically turns ideas, evidence, tasks, and systems into structure.

## Core Form

- Simple paper box body with thick black marker outline.
- Littlebox body is white or near-white negative space on the selected pale background. Do not fill the body with tan, kraft cardboard brown, beige, or shaded material color.
- Default view is **front-left three-quarter view**: the front panel is the largest visible face, one side panel is visible, and the closed top lid is visible.
- Two small dot eyes always sit on the front panel, horizontally centered, never on a side panel, lid, object, or label.
- Tiny thin legs always attach under the front panel.
- Two small twig arms are the default action limbs. They are short thin black curved branches emerging from the left and right side seams, never from the front face, lid, eyes, or props.
- Slight dents, imperfect perspective, and handmade wobble are allowed, but the box must remain the same character.
- Lines must be fat-marker, rough, slightly wobbly, and visibly hand-drawn. Avoid ruler-straight vector edges.
- Calm, deadpan, serious expression. No mouth, eyebrows, smile, blush, or exaggerated cartoon face.

Littlebox can be a light paper box or a sturdier carton, but final article illustrations keep it closed.

## Canonical Arm Rule

- Default to two small side-seam twig arms when Littlebox handles, carries, pushes, catches, points at, or holds anything.
- Littlebox has **at most two arms total**: one left side-seam twig arm and one right side-seam twig arm. Never draw a third arm, lower arm, spare arm, tail-like helper line, or extra black limb attached to the body.
- Arms are simple single black strokes, thinner than the body outline but with the same wobbly marker texture.
- Arms emerge around the side seams at mid-height, like little branches from both sides of the box.
- Arms may curve around a prop or touch it, but do not add hands, gloves, fingers, sleeves, or mitten shapes.
- If one arm is hidden by perspective or by a prop, keep the visible arm attached to a side seam and avoid front-facing stick arms.
- If Littlebox needs to sort three or more targets, only two arms may touch objects. Route the remaining targets with external trays, slots, amber paths, falling tags, or nearby props. Do not solve multi-target actions by adding limbs.

## Canonical Tape Rule

Littlebox has **exactly one amber tape strip**. The tape is the strongest identity marker after the box body and dot eyes. Do not let it wander.

### Tape Geometry

- Tape is a narrow amber seam strip, about 10%-15% of the top width.
- Tape sits exactly on the central split line between the two top flaps.
- Tape direction is front-to-back across the top, perpendicular to the front edge.
- In the default three-quarter view, the tape may read as one centered strip or as two close amber marker strokes on the same strip; do not treat them as separate tapes.
- Tape has a short front tab hanging slightly over the top front edge.
- The front tab has an irregular sawtooth / jagged torn cut, usually 2-3 small teeth, like hand-torn packing tape.
- The jagged front tab is the most reliable tape cue. Preserve it whenever the front lip is visible.
- Tape is outlined in black marker when large enough.
- Tape never covers a whole flap, never appears as a large orange rectangle, and never becomes an arrow.

### Closed Tape

- Tape follows the **central lid seam** where the two top flaps meet.
- In the default three-quarter view, it appears as one centered amber strip running front-to-back across the top seam, with a short jagged torn tab visible over the front lip.
- The jagged cut should face the viewer; avoid a smooth rectangular tab.
- It must not appear randomly on a side wall, face panel, back panel, loose card, path arrow, or as several unrelated tape patches.

### Retired Tape States

Opened-flap tape variants are retired. Final Littlebox illustrations keep the lid closed and show the centered closed-seam tape with the jagged front tab. Do not draw tape on any raised flap because raised flaps should not appear.

## Canonical Lid Rule

- Littlebox is closed-only in final article illustrations.
- Two top flaps meet cleanly along one central seam.
- The central seam runs front-to-back across the top plane, perpendicular to the front edge.
- The lid seam and jagged front tape tab must stay visible when Littlebox is large enough.
- Do not draw raised flaps, open flaps, half-open flaps, cutaways, visible interiors, or any main opening.
- Never make the box look like a laptop, treasure chest, toolbox, pizza box, bucket, tray, shopping bag, crate, or mailbox.

## Canonical Proportions

- Front panel is a simple rectangle, slightly wider than tall.
- Side panel is narrower than the front panel and visible on the viewer's left or right.
- Eyes sit on the upper-middle area of the front panel, not too low and not on the lid.
- Legs are two short vertical black strokes under the front panel, roughly below the eyes.
- The box should be compact and squat, not a tall cube, long shipping carton, or flat tray.
- The front panel should feel like a character face, not blank packaging. Keep the two eyes visually central and readable.

## Deformation Limits

Littlebox can act, but the body should not become the tool.

- Do not stretch Littlebox into a long rectangle, conveyor, press machine, tray, bridge slab, tube, or anonymous package.
- Keep the front panel roughly 1.2-1.6x wider than tall.
- If an idea needs compression, use a separate press plate, heavy slip, squeezed image sheet, or output package; Littlebox remains the operator.
- If an idea needs routing or handoff, use an external amber path; Littlebox remains a compact sorting gate.
- If an idea needs bridging, Littlebox may hold or place a bridge object; Littlebox should not become the bridge itself.
- Arms, paths, arrows, and props may stretch; the box body may not.

## Closed-Only State

Use the closed state for all ideas, including:

- preserving structure
- carrying output
- packaging a conclusion
- protecting evidence
- delivering a result
- storing a system
- stabilizing chaos
- collecting input
- sorting chaos
- filtering
- compression
- handoff
- conversion
- revision
- moving from messy to usable

Visual cues: closed lid, two top flaps meeting at the central seam, one amber tape strip centered along that seam, a jagged torn front tab, side-seam twig arms doing the action, and external slips/stacks/paths/props showing intake, sorting, filtering, compression, or handoff.

## Personality

- serious but slightly awkward
- useful without being cheerful
- inventive, quiet, and dry
- works like a low-tech system operator
- never performs for cuteness

## Action Vocabulary

Use Littlebox to:

- collect, catch, hold, carry
- close, seal, stamp
- sort, fold, compress, bundle
- bridge, block, route, redirect
- hide, reveal, store, deliver
- spill, overflow, recover, repair

## Failure Modes

Regenerate or rewrite if:

- Littlebox only stands beside the idea
- side-seam twig arms are missing in an action scene where Littlebox holds, pushes, carries, catches, or points at something
- Littlebox has more than two arms, a lower third arm, a tail-like attached action line, or extra limb-like strokes connected to the body
- arms emerge from the front face, lid, eyes, or prop instead of the side seams
- arms become thick, gloved, fingered, sleeved, or mascot-like
- the amber tape appears in a different place from image to image
- the tape is not aligned with the central lid seam
- the tape is too wide or covers an entire flap
- the tape lacks the jagged torn front tab when the front lip is visible
- the front tab becomes a smooth rectangular sticker instead of a sawtooth torn tape end
- Littlebox is open, half-open, cutaway, or shows any visible interior
- any top flap is raised or separated from the closed lid plane
- the lid direction suggests a laptop, toolbox, treasure chest, pizza box, bucket, tray, shopping bag, crate, or mailbox
- eyes move off the front panel
- the box is drawn from a random angle that hides the canonical front face
- the body is stretched into a long carton, tray, bridge, machine, or output object
- it looks like a delivery-company icon
- it looks like a clean vector logistics box
- it becomes tan/brown realistic cardboard
- the outline is thin, smooth, or ruler-straight
- linework looks auto-traced, thresholded, posterized, or digitally cleaned instead of hand-drawn marker
- it becomes a smiling cute box mascot
- it has expressive cartoon facial features
- it wears clothes or accessories
- it is rendered as realistic cardboard
- removing Littlebox would leave the core metaphor unchanged

## Recognition Rule

Across transformations, preserve at least four of these markers:

- box body
- dot eyes
- canonical front-left three-quarter view
- closed top flaps
- tiny legs
- side-seam twig arms when acting
- single amber seam tape with jagged front tab

For final article images, preserve at least five markers. The dot eyes, compact box body, side-seam twig arms when acting, and jagged amber front tab are non-negotiable when visible.
